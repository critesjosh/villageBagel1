Our Company.
The life of a traditional bagel is short. Move quickly and get them while they're hot!

Local owner and bagel connoisseur, Connie Leaf, grew up just outside New York City, where every neighborhood has its own family-run bagel shop. Connie's favorite childhood memories are of her cheery Grandfather walking through the front door every Saturday, brown paper bag in hand, filled with a variety of hot bagels and homemade shmears from the local bagelry. The bagel is to New York City what the croissant is to Paris. Ask any New Yorker living in Colorado: the majority of bagels you find in this state are just steamed, donut-shaped rolls, and simply NWTC (not worth the calories).

Placeholder. Once you try an authentic bagel, you'll never eat the store bought ones again.

Village Bagel provides the Vail Valley with traditionally prepared bagels made locally by East Coast transplants. Leaf and partner spent months experimenting with different recipes, water solutions, and oven temperatures to bring the New York bagel experience to the Rocky Mountains. Chewy on the inside, crunchy exterior, and a rich, developed flavor made possible by a 24-hour fermentation process, eight simple ingredients, and a whole lotta love and passion hand-rolled into every bagel.

Call us today.

Village Bagel celebrates and represents tradition. We are committed to simple ingredients and old-world processes. We hope to be an integral part of the local community, building lasting memories and passing on traditions like the ones we have from our childhood in New York. We are your neighborhood bagel shop, blending the East Coast breakfast culture with a Colorado spirit, and spreading happiness one warm bagel at a time.
